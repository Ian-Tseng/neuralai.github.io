# NeuralAI Design System

## Design Position

NeuralAI should look like a legitimate stealth technical startup: clear, research-grounded, and credible without sounding overbuilt or corporate.

The most important first impression is: NeuralAI is building AI from neuroscience, with concrete technical targets such as architecture, learning rules, and memory mechanisms.

## Information Architecture

- Public pages: Home, Technology, Contact.
- Home should establish the company and what it is building.
- Technology should carry the technical explanation and avoid campaign or agency positioning.
- Contact should provide email, domain, company information, and concise team information when useful.
- Do not reintroduce a separate Founder page unless the user asks for it.

## Visual Direction

- Aesthetic: clean research startup, not SaaS marketing gloss.
- Use a restrained white and blue system with enough green accent to signal science and progress.
- Prefer precise diagrams, network imagery, and sparse technical structure over decorative illustrations.
- Avoid generic AI buzzword visuals, dark cyber gradients, and excessive card stacking.

## Typography

- Primary font stack: `Inter, ui-sans-serif, system-ui, -apple-system, BlinkMacSystemFont, "Segoe UI", Arial, sans-serif`.
- Headings should be plain and direct.
- Avoid clever, abstract, or overly promotional headings.
- Good examples: `NeuralAI Technology`, `Contact NeuralAI`, `NeuralAI builds neuroscience-based AI.`

## Color Tokens

- Ink: `#102033`
- Muted text: `#54657a`
- Line: `#dce7f2`
- Paper: `#ffffff`
- Soft background: `#f5f9fc`
- Primary blue: `#064d8f`
- Secondary blue: `#0c7fd4`
- Accent green: `#19a88f`

## Layout Rules

- Keep navigation minimal: Home, Technology, Contact.
- Use full-width sections with constrained content, not nested cards.
- Cards are acceptable only for repeated capabilities or contact details.
- Keep section headings plain and useful.
- Make `neuralai_logo/neuralai_default_icon.png` the header icon unless the user selects another asset.
- Current header trial is icon-only, without a visible wordmark, because the page content already names NeuralAI clearly.

## Content Rules

- Canonical contact email: `iantseng@neuralai.com.tw`.
- Canonical domain: `neuralai.com.tw`.
- Origin/status: NeuralAI is a stealth startup based on an idea that began in `2019`; do not describe 2019 as legal founding unless the user confirms incorporation.
- Website history: the NeuralAI website/domain has existed since `2021`; the current static GitHub Pages site is a rebuild.
- Contact purposes: cooperation, technical collaboration, neuroscience-based AI pilots, architecture discussions, research, and related ideas.
- Do not advertise internships or hiring unless budget and role details are explicitly confirmed.
- Public-facing company wording should describe what NeuralAI builds, not announce that it is a startup.
- Preferred phrasing: `NeuralAI builds AI from neuroscience.`
- Keep 2019 idea history, 2021 domain history, and rebuild context out of the primary homepage copy unless the user asks for an About/history section.
- Technical description should mention architecture, learning rules, and memory mechanisms.
- Technology copy can mention neuron units and interaction models when describing simulation work.
- Avoid stale positioning around ad refinement, agency partnerships, or campaign optimization unless explicitly reintroduced.

## Copy Standard

- Keep copy plain first.
- Use persuasion checklists such as CLASSR only as a light review tool, not as a formula to force into every line.
- Prefer short sentences with real objects: brain, signal, memory, learning rule, model, architecture.
- Use a story arc only where it naturally helps clarity.
- Avoid abstract filler such as `innovative`, `next-generation`, `cutting-edge`, or `unlock`.
- Make body text large enough to read comfortably: base text around 18px, important explanatory copy around 20-24px.
